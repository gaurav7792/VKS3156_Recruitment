**State 3156 · Internal Use Only**

> This is a historical guide. Processes may change at any time.

## 1. Ordinary Invites

### 1.1 Allocation

The state receives 35 ordinary invites each transfer event, distributed as follows:

| Pool | Details |
|---|---|
| Alliance Allocated (30 total) | 5 invites per alliance, across all alliances in the coalition |
| Unallocated (5 total) | Open pool — any alliance may claim. First-come, first-served upon request to President. |

Based on the assumption that there are 6 alliances > 500M in Prep.

### 1.2 Approval Process

Ordinary invites operate on a no-filter basis:

- Any alliance submits a transfer request to the President (or designated Transfer Manager).
- Request is approved automatically — no vote required.
- Invite is issued once the President confirms the applicant meets the in-game power cap set for the event.

> **NOTE:** Alliances are responsible for vetting their own members before submitting requests. Submitting a request is an alliance endorsement of that player.

## 2. Special Invites

### 2.1 Eligibility Criteria

A player must meet **both** of the following requirements simultaneously. Either alone is insufficient.

| Leaderboard | Logic | Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Labyrinth Leaderboard | AND | Top 10 in the state |
| Top Hero Power Leaderboard | AND | Top 10 in the state |

The intent is to recruit genuine whales and dolphins — players who are both high-power and show decent game IQ. Ranking on one leaderboard alone does not meet the bar.

> **NOTE:** Both leaderboard standings must be verified at the time of request, not at transfer time.

### 2.2 Invite Pool and Regeneration

Special invites are a limited, slow-regenerating resource. The coalition spends them carefully.

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| Starting pool | 3 special invites |
| Regen rate | +1 per transfer event (not a full reset to 3) |
| Example use | 2 this event → 1 remaining, then 2 available next event |
| Maximum pool | Capped at 3 (unused invites do not stack beyond cap) |

> **NOTE:** Burning 2–3 invites costs multiple events to recover. Every special invite goes to a coalition vote because it is a shared resource — no single alliance decides unilaterally.

### 2.3 Approval Process

Special invites require a coalition-wide vote:

1. Requesting alliance submits the candidate to the President with leaderboard evidence.
2. President opens a vote among alliance leaders.
3. Majority vote required to approve. In the event of a tie, the President casts the deciding vote.
4. President issues the special invite upon approval.

> **NOTE:** The President's tiebreaker authority is final. No appeals process exists beyond coalition consensus.

## 3. End-to-End Transfer Process

### Phase I — Pre-Transfer (3 Days)

- President sets Transfer Power Cap for the state.
- Alliances identify candidates and notify the President of intended requests.
- Ordinary candidates: confirm they are under the power cap.
- Special candidates: gather and submit leaderboard screenshots to their R4/R5.

### Phase II — Invitational Transfer (2 Days)

- Alliance leaders submit ordinary requests to the President.
- Unallocated invite requests submitted on a first-come, first-served basis.
- Special invite candidates are voted on by alliance leaders during this window.
- President issues invites once approvals are confirmed.
- Incoming players disband troops from Enlistment Office if required, then accept invite.

### Phase III — Open Transfer (2 Days)

- Remaining spots are open to any player meeting the power cap.
- No invite required during this phase — players self-transfer.
- President and Former President cannot transfer during this phase.

## 4. Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| President | Sets power cap; issues all invites; runs special invite vote; holds tiebreaker authority; tracks invite usage. |
| Alliance Leaders (R4/R5) | Vet candidates internally; submit requests to President; cast votes on special invite decisions; manage their 5-invite allocation. |
| Transfer Candidate | Confirms power cap eligibility; leaves current alliance before transfer; accepts invite within the event window. |
